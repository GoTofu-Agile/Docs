## 🎤 Presentation Outline: The Pivot

**1. 🚀 Where We Started *(~30 sec)***

- Inspired by a **Y Combinator post**: *"What if there was a Cursor for Product Managers?"*
- Goal: build an AI assistant that solves core PM pain points
- Kicked off with **user research** — conducted interviews with diverse PMs (fintech, software, hardware, etc.)

---

**2. 🔍 What We Found *(~45 sec)***

- Pain points were **real but scattered** — fragmented info, context-switching, lack of tooling
- However: **no clear pattern emerged** across PM types
- A single solution couldn't meaningfully address the majority of pain points
- Conclusion: the original problem space was **too broad to build a focused product on**

---

**3. 💡 The Pivot Moment *(~45 sec)***

- During our research process, one team member had **built a tool** to generate synthetic personas for interviews
- Used it to run **AI-simulated user interviews** — and it worked surprisingly well
- Our supervisor's reaction: *"This could be a product on its own"*
- Key insight: **idea validation** is a pain point not just for PMs, but for **anyone building something**

---

**4. 🎯 Our New Direction *(~45 sec)***

- We're now building a **synthetic user interview platform**
- Users describe their idea → AI generates realistic personas → conducts interviews → surfaces insights
- Faster, cheaper, and more accessible than traditional user research
- Addresses a **validated, widespread need**: early-stage idea validation

---

**5. 🥊 Differentiation *(~30 sec)***

- Yes, competitors exist — but the space is **early and fragmented**
- Our edge: born from *real research frustration*, not just a tech demo
- Focus on **quality of persona realism** and **actionable output**, not just synthetic chat
- Built by a team that has *lived* the problem
