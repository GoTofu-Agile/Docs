[https://manuelohan.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/05/The-Mom-Test-en.pdf](https://manuelohan.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/05/The-Mom-Test-en.pdf)

### **Key Principles of The Mom Test**

- **Don't Pitch, Just Listen:** The goal is to understand, not to convince. Keep discussions focused on their problems, not your solution.
- **Avoid "Yes" or "No" Questions:** Instead of asking "Would you use this?", ask "How do you currently handle [problem]?".
- **Focus on the Past, Not Future:** Future-based questions ("Would you buy...?") lead to polite lies. Past-based questions ("When was the last time you...?") provide evidence of behavior.
- **Get Specifics:** Ask for concrete examples. If they say they "always" do something, ask for the last time it happened.
- **Ignore Compliments:** Compliments are often polite lies. Deflect them and steer the conversation back to specific, often boring, facts about their current workflow.
- **Look for Commitment:** If they are not willing to commit (time, money, or a reputation-risking intro) to solving the problem, it is not a real problem for them.

**Questions to Avoid vs. Ask**

- ❌ **Bad:** "Do you think this is a good idea?" (Invites polite, false affirmation).
- ✅ **Good:** "Tell me about the last time you faced this problem?" (Reveals real behavior).
- ❌ **Bad:** "Would you pay for...?" (Hypothetical, unproven).
- ✅ **Good:** "How much are you currently paying to solve this?" (Shows actual monetary value).

**Conducting the Interview**

- **Keep it Casual:** Treat it as a conversation, not a structured interview, to reduce pressure.
- **Ask "Why" to Discover Motivation:** If they mention a problem, ask why it's a problem, or why it matters, to uncover the underlying pain point.
- **Keep it Brief:** The best insights often come in a 15-20 minute focused conversation rather than a long, formal meeting.
