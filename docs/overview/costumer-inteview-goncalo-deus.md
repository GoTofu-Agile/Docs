# 🎯 Interview Summary – Gonçalo (Financial Product Manager, Cofidis)

---

# 1️⃣ Executive Summary (TL;DR)

Gonçalo works in a **highly regulated fintech environment** where:

- Product decisions are mostly **top-down (CEO / management-driven)**
- Innovation is heavily constrained by **regulation**
- AI tools are **not allowed**
- Most of his work (≈70%) is **stakeholder alignment**
- Insight → feature translation is manual and slow
- Information loss happens frequently (email-based coordination)

⚠️ Key takeaway:

The core pain is **not “What should we build?”**, but:

“How do I align everyone, structure insights, and avoid losing information in a slow, complex organization?”

---

# 2️⃣ Role & Context

**Company Context**

- Large French fintech (Cofidis)
- Highly regulated (Bank of Portugal + EU rules)
- Data-sensitive environment
- No external AI allowed

**Team Setup**

- Kanban
- 8-person product team (2 FPMs)
- Engineers are centralized in a digital department
- Priorities come from management, not PMs

**Role Definition**

Gonçalo is a **bridge** between:

- Management (strategy & targets)
- Marketing
- Legal
- Operations
- Developers

He does **not** own full product direction.

---

# 3️⃣ How Decisions Are Made

## 🏢 Top-Down Structure

- CEO defines target segments
- PM team builds features to support those targets
- Regulation heavily constrains options

Quote insight:

“The decision of what to do next is not basically ours.”

✅ Important: They are not struggling to “discover new ideas.”

❌ They are executing predefined directions.

---

# 4️⃣ Customer Insights Process

## Data Sources

- Dedicated customer insights team
- Focus groups (Amazon vouchers as incentive)
- 1:1 interviews with large clients
- Yearly NPS survey
- Daily dashboards (quantitative data)

## B2B Context

Mainly B2B clients (companies using their financial product).

---

## 🔎 Translation Process (Critical Insight)

After collecting feedback:

- They gather all ideas
- Use Post-its
- Brainstorm manually
- Try to identify patterns
- Prioritize based on discussion

Quote:

“We just grab all the information… we go to our room, we just make post-its.”

⚠️ Pain Point:
It is hard to judge which ideas actually matter.
Timing biases survey results.
Manual synthesis → risk of missing ideas.

---

# 5️⃣ Major Pain Points (Ranked)

## 🥇 1. Stakeholder Alignment (Biggest Pain)

He estimates:

- 30% product thinking
- 70% alignment & coordination

For one feature he must talk to:

- Legal
- Operations
- Developers
- Finance
- Multiple internal teams

Quote:

“To align everybody usually takes two weeks.”

Coordination method:

- Email
- Excel checklists
- Manual tracking

⚠️ Information loss happens frequently.

---

## 🥈 2. Manual Insight Synthesis

- Brainstorming is slow
- Hundreds of ideas
- Hard to see priorities
- Long cycles (Nov → March before execution)

Quote:

“Maybe we miss some ideas because of the way we do it.”

---

## 🥉 3. Organizational Slowness

- Small feature takes 2–3 months
- Meeting-heavy culture
- Many dependencies

Quote:

“To build one thing, it takes like two, three months.”

---

## 4️⃣ AI Constraint

AI is forbidden due to:

- Data security
- Regulation
- Company policy

He explicitly said:

“If I could use AI, I would be so much happier.”

But:
Even if your product exists — he cannot use it.

⚠️ Market constraint insight:
Regulated fintech PMs may not be your early adopters.

---

# 6️⃣ Where Is the Real Friction?

This is crucial for your thesis.

**❌ NOT the Core Problem:**

- Lack of ideas
- Lack of data
- Lack of customer contact

**✅ Real Problems:**

- Prioritization across many inputs
- Stakeholder alignment complexity
- Information fragmentation
- Manual coordination
- Slow organizational execution

This is an **organizational systems problem**, not an idea-generation problem.

---

# 7️⃣ Jobs-to-be-Done Analysis

When Gonçalo runs customer interviews, he wants to:

“Turn a chaotic set of insights into a structured, prioritized roadmap that everyone agrees on.”

When building features, he wants to:

“Coordinate many teams without losing information.”

When working daily, he wants to:

“Reduce manual overhead and move faster.”

---

# 8️⃣ Signals Relevant for “Cursor for PMs”

Your hypothesis:
AI that turns insights → structured feature proposals.

What validated:

✅ Manual synthesis is painful

✅ Prioritization is difficult

✅ Brainstorming is inefficient

What challenges your idea:

❌ Decisions are top-down

❌ Compliance constraints dominate

❌ AI usage is forbidden

❌ Alignment > discovery

---

# 9️⃣ Strategic Implications for Your Team

You need to decide:

## Option A – Stay with Discovery AI

Target:

- Startups
- Tech-native PMs
- Less regulated environments

## Option B – Pivot Toward Alignment Tool

Build something that:

- Centralizes stakeholder communication
- Tracks decisions
- Structures dependencies
- Prevents information loss
- Makes complex enterprise alignment visible

Because his biggest pain is not:
“What to build?”

But:
“How to get it built in this organization?”
