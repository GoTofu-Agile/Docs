# Interview Questionnaire

*v2 — February 2026*

## Part 1 — Context & Role Setup

1. What is your current title, and how would you describe your role in one sentence?
2. What kind of product do you work on? (B2B SaaS, internal tooling, B2C, platform, hardware-adjacent, etc.)
3. How large is your immediate team? (Devs, designers, QA, BI, etc.)
4. How large is the overall organization? (Startup <50, Scale-up 50–500, Enterprise 500+)
5. Do you operate in a specific framework? (SAFe, Scrum, Kanban, Shape Up, or something custom?)
6. Who do you report to? (CTO, VP Product, CPO, etc.)

---

## Part 2 — Day-to-Day Workflow & Time Allocation

1. Walk me through yesterday (or a recent typical day). What did you do from start to finish?
2. How many meetings do you have on an average day? Roughly how many are:
    - Recurring/cadence-based (standups, sprint ceremonies, syncs)?
    - Ad hoc (firefighting, stakeholder requests)?
    - Customer/stakeholder-facing?
3. If I followed you for a week with a stopwatch, what single activity would consume the most time?
4. What percentage of your week do you estimate you spend on each:
    - Communication & stakeholder management
    - Documentation (PRDs, specs, meeting notes)
    - Prioritization & planning
    - Discovery & research
    - Reporting (upward, cross-functional)
    - Process improvement / "meta-work"
5. How much of your time feels productive vs. overhead? What's the ratio?

---

## Part 3 — Documentation & Information Flow

1. What does your PRD (or equivalent spec document) creation process look like, step by step?
    - Where do the inputs come from? (Customer calls, Slack, email, handwritten notes, transcripts?)
    - How long does it take from first input to final signed-off document?
2. How many distinct information sources do you typically pull from to create a single PRD or feature spec?
3. Do you use LLMs or AI tools today to help with documentation?
    - If yes: Which ones? What works well? What doesn't?
    - If no: Why not? What's blocking adoption?
4. Once a PRD is written, how do you ensure it stays the "source of truth" as things evolve during development?
5. How often do misunderstandings or miscommunications between you and developers trace back to documentation gaps? Can you give me a specific recent example?

---

## Part 4 — Prioritization & Decision-Making

1. How do you currently prioritize what to build next?
    - Do you use a formal framework? (RICE, WSJF, Impact/Effort matrix, etc.)
    - How do you measure impact? (Dollar value, user metrics, strategic alignment?)
    - How do you measure effort? (Story points, t-shirt sizes, dev-days?)
2. How often is your data-driven prioritization overruled by a subjective decision from leadership? When that happens, how does it make you feel? What's the consequence?
3. Can you tell me about a time you made a prioritization call that turned out to be wrong? How did you discover it was wrong? How long did it take?
4. If a tool could recommend what to prioritize based on your goals, constraints, and data — would you trust it? What would it need to include for you to trust it?
5. How do you connect individual tickets/stories back to quarterly or company-level goals today?

---

## Part 5 — Stakeholder Communication & Transparency

1. Who are your key stakeholders, and how do you communicate progress to each of them? Do you tailor the format/language for different audiences?
2. How often do stakeholders ping you just to ask "what's the status?" If you could eliminate those status-check interruptions, how much time would you save per week?
3. Do your stakeholders/customers have any visibility into your Jira board or project tracking tool? If not: Why not? What would need to be true for you to give them access?
4. Describe a recent situation where miscommunication between you and a stakeholder caused a real problem (wasted sprint, wrong feature built, delayed launch, etc.).
5. If there were an automated way to send proactive status updates to stakeholders — what would it need to include to be genuinely useful (not just noise)?

---

## Part 6 — Discovery & Customer Insight

1. How do you currently gather customer/user insights? (Direct interviews, analytics, secondhand from sales/support, session recordings, surveys?)
2. How many degrees of separation are there between you and your end user?
3. When you can't talk to customers directly, what proxies do you use? How reliable are they? Can you share an example where a proxy insight turned out to be wrong?
4. What tools do you use for quantitative user data? (Google Analytics, Amplitude, Mixpanel, Hotjar, etc.) Are there tools you wish you had but don't, and why?
5. How do you validate a hypothesis or proposed solution before committing development resources?

---

## Part 7 — Reporting & Upward Management

1. How do you report progress and outcomes to leadership? What format? How often? How long does preparation take?
2. Does the way you report change depending on the audience? (e.g., story points for CTO, dollar impact for CFO)
3. Have you tried automating any part of reporting? What happened?
4. If a tool auto-generated a weekly report from your Jira/project data — would you use it as-is, or would you need to heavily edit it? What's missing from auto-generated reports?

---

## Part 8 — Tooling, Procurement & Adoption Barriers

1. What is your current core tool stack? (Jira, Confluence, Slack, Notion, Linear, etc.)
2. Is there a tool you've wanted to adopt but couldn't? What blocked it? (Budget, legal/compliance, IT approval, lack of integration?)
3. How long does it typically take to get a new tool approved and deployed in your organization?
4. Are there tools your team is paying for but barely using?
5. If a new PM tool had to integrate with only 3 tools in your stack to be useful — which 3 would they be?

---

## Part 9 — The "Magic Wand" & Willingness to Pay

1. If you had a magic wand and could eliminate one recurring frustration from your workweek, what would it be?
2. If an AI agent could take over one part of your job entirely — which part would you hand off first?
3. What would that agent need to do perfectly for you to trust it?
4. Is there anything about how you work — the hard parts, the weird parts — that you've never told anyone, but I should know?
5. Would you (or your company) pay for a tool that solved top pain point they identified? Roughly what would it be worth per seat/month?
6. Who would need to approve the purchase of such a tool in your org?
